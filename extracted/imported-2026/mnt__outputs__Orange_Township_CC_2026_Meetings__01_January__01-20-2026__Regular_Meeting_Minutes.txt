City of Orange Township – City Council
 Regular Meeting Minutes – January 20, 2026
MINUTES CITY COUNCIL – CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP, NEW JERSEY
THIS IS THE REGULAR MEETING OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP HELD AT CITY
HALL, 29 NORTH DAY STREET, ORANGE, NEW JERSEY ON TUESDAY, JANUARY 20, 2026 FOLLOWING THE
CONFERENCE MEETING.
Councilmember Clifford R. Ross led all assembled in the pledge of allegiance to the flag.
ROLL CALL
Present: Councilmember Kerry J. Coley, East Ward; Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert, West Ward; Council Vice
President Weldon M. Montague, III, At-Large; Councilmember Clifford R. Ross, At-Large; Councilmember Jamie B.
Summers-Johnson, South Ward; Council President Adrienne K. Wooten, At-Large.
Also Present: Trisha A. Scipio, Deputy Municipal Clerk; Chris M. Hartwyk, Business Administrator; Derrick Brown, Fire
Chief; Nile S. Clements, Chief Financial Officer; Gracia R. Montilus, Esq. City Attorney; Marlin G. Townes, Legislative
Research Officer.
Absent: Councilmember Tency A. Eason, North Ward.
APPROVAL OF MEETING MINUTES
January 6, 2026 Conference and Regular Meeting Minutes adopted. Motion Adopted.
REPORTS
DEPARTMENT MONTHLY REVENUE COLLECTION: The Clerk's Office collected $1,125.00 for December 2025.
POSTPONED ORDINANCES
74-2025: Motion to adopt Ordinance 74-2025. Roll Call – Yeas: All present. Motion Adopted.
30-2026WO: Transfer of Appropriation Reserves (postponed from 1/6/2026). Motion Adopted.
CITIZEN COMMENTS
Alysa Rodriguez of 487 Mountainview Avenue addressed Tax Abatement, Resolution 60-2026, the election process, the
2026 Budget, homeowner advocacy, and low-income housing.
Kelly Ann Joseph and David Joseph of 660 Nassau Street spoke regarding parking issues, signage, and concerns related to
433 Lincoln Avenue.
Dr. Gloria Boseman of 345 Olcott Street addressed Citizens' Comments time limitations, housing development, the
Developers Trust Fund, Board and Committee appointments, and the Temporary Budget.
COUNCIL COMMENTS
Councilmember Clifford R. Ross inquired about matters related to the Highland Avenue Train Station.
Councilmember Kerry J. Coley raised questions regarding the sidewalk near the former lodge, ongoing development, and
parking meters. He also commented on the aesthetic appeal of the new community center.
Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert expressed appreciation for the prayers and well wishes she received and offered
congratulations to members of the Fire and Police Departments on their recent promotions.
Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson spoke about the observance of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., highlighted the
positive accomplishments of an Orange student, and requested the installation of "Welcome to Orange" signage.
Council Vice President Weldon M. Montague, III thanked attendees for the well wishes and inquired about Parking Utility
community benefits and matters related to the Department of Public Works (DPW).
