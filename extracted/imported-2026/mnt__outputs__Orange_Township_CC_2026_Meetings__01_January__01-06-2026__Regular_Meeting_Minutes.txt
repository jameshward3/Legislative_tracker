City of Orange Township – City Council
 Regular Meeting Minutes – January 6, 2026
MINUTES CITY COUNCIL – CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP, NEW JERSEY
THIS IS THE REGULAR MEETING OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP HELD AT CITY
HALL, 29 NORTH DAY STREET, ORANGE, NEW JERSEY ON TUESDAY, JANUARY 6, 2026 FOLLOWING THE
CONFERENCE MEETING.
Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert led all assembled in the pledge of allegiance to the flag.
ROLL CALL
Present: Councilmember Kerry J. Coley, East Ward; Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert, West Ward; Councilmember
Jamie B. Summers-Johnson, South Ward; Council President Adrienne K. Wooten, At-Large.
Also Present: Trisha A. Scipio, Deputy Municipal Clerk; Chris M. Hartwyk, Business Administrator; Derrick Brown, Fire
Chief; Nile Clements, Chief Financial Officer; Brian Mooney, Police Chief; Gracia R. Montilus, Esq. City Attorney; Marlin G.
Townes, Legislative Research Officer.
Absent: Councilmember Tency A. Eason, North Ward; Council Vice President Weldon M. Montague, III, At-Large;
Councilmember Clifford R. Ross, At-Large.
APPROVAL OF MEETING MINUTES
A motion to adopt the minutes from the December 16, 2025 Conference and Regular Meeting was made by Councilmember
Kerry J. Coley and seconded by Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson. Motion Adopted.
REPORTS
DEPARTMENT MONTHLY REVENUE COLLECTION: The Health Department collected $6,982.00 for November 2025;
Community Service collected $7,082.00 for November 2025.
OPRA REPORTS: For December 2025 there were 115 requested, 33 completed, and 82 remaining.
CITIZEN COMMENTS
Brent Engle of 174 Heywood Avenue expressed concerns about Resolution 551-2025 and the reason the bid is being
rejected.
Dr. Gloria Boseman of 345 Olcott Street expressed concerns about time restrictions for Citizens Comments, Housing
Development, Developers Trust Fund, Board and Committee appointments, and the Temporary Budget.
Kelly Ann Joseph and David Joseph of 660 Nassau Street expressed concerns about the Brook with West Orange, Permit
Parking, safety issues, Landlord accountability, Developments, the increase in the Water Bill, and concerns about 433
Lincoln Avenue.
COUNCIL COMMENTS
Councilmember Kerry J. Coley wished all a Happy New Year. He inquired about Developments, the YMCA, the Hospital
project and the Aspire application.
Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert mentioned a light out on Henry and West Essex, the new traffic signs by Lincoln
Elementary School, the upcoming West Ward Meeting on January 21st, and she is looking forward to the paving season.
Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson expressed heartfelt condolences to Councilmember Tency A. Eason. She
spoke about the paving schedule, 433 Lincoln Avenue, Orange Center, programming for students, partnering with the
County, Claremont Terrace traffic pattern, and upcoming traffic change on Nassau.
Council President Adrienne K. Wooten wished all a Happy New Year then asked the City Attorney to explain the bidding
process. She mentioned Robo Calls, thanked Police and Fire for their role in 3 Kings Day, and spoke about Community
Meeting on January 28th and Youth Town Hall on January 18th.
