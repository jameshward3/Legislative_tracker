City of Orange Township – City Council
 Conference Meeting Minutes – April 7, 2026
MINUTES CITY COUNCIL – CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP, NEW JERSEY
THIS IS A CONFERENCE MEETING OF THE MUNICIPAL COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP HELD AT
CITY HALL, 29 NORTH DAY STREET, ORANGE, NEW JERSEY ON TUESDAY, APRIL 7, 2026 AT 7:00PM.
The Local Girl Scout Troop led all assembled in the pledge of allegiance to the flag.
ROLL CALL
Present: Councilmember Kerry J. Coley, East Ward; Councilmember Tency A. Eason, North Ward; Councilmember
Quantavia L. Hilbert, West Ward; Council Vice President Weldon M. Montague, III, At-Large; Councilmember Clifford R.
Ross, At-Large; Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson, South Ward (Arrived at 7:04pm); Council President Adrienne
K. Wooten, At-Large (Arrived at 7:18pm).
Also Present: Trisha A. Scipio, Acting Municipal Clerk; Chris M. Hartwyk, Business Administrator; Derrick Brown, Fire Chief;
Brian Mooney, Police Chief; Gracia R. Montilus, Esq. City Attorney; Marlin G. Townes, Legislative Research Officer.
MATTERS FOR DISCUSSION
Radium Girls Presentation
Margaret Cunningham appeared before the Council to request continued support for the next phase of the Radium Girls
memorial project — "Radium Girls Memoriam Part 2." This phase will include the installation of a sundial at College Park.
Dallis Whitley provided additional details regarding the proposed sundial installation. Councilmembers Tency A. Eason and
Clifford R. Ross thanked the representatives for their presentation and will continue to support the cause.
LEGISLATION DISCUSSION
The Acting Municipal Clerk announced the resolutions and ordinances to be presented at tonight's meeting.
223-2026: Resolution rescinding proposed Ordinance No. 2 and establishing a policy resolution to reaffirm compliance with
N.J.S.A. 18A:41-7.1 regarding Critical Incident Mapping Data for School Safety. Councilmember Eason questioned this
resolution as the Ordinance mentioned had never been approved. Sending back to Administration.
3-2026: An ordinance authorizing online payment of property maintenance summons. Councilmember Hilbert asked for
details; Councilmember Ross spoke about the system of online payments.
201-2026: Resolution amending Resolution No. 335-2025 to authorize additional emergency funding to FF1 Apparatus, LLC
for fire apparatus maintenance, not to exceed $70,000.00. Councilmember Ross inquired about the urgency (mechanical
break).
203-2026: A resolution rescinding Resolution 183-2026 (Morton Salt additional rock salt funds). Councilmember Ross asked
why this is being rescinded — the Certification of Funds was not provided at the time of adoption.
204-2026: A resolution authorizing Watermen, LLC for professional engineering services for CDBG 2025 Central Avenue
Park Rehabilitation of Tennis Courts, not to exceed $18,300.00.
205-2026: Extension of contract with Remington & Vernick Engineers for Brook Alley Garage Improvement Project, not to
exceed $15,131.23.
210-2026: A resolution authorizing Mott MacDonald for professional engineering services for the citywide Lead Service Line
Replacement Program, not to exceed $1,214,000.00.
213-2026: Application for NJDEP grant funding for remedial action at 110 North Day Street, Colgate Park, in the amount of
$225,109.50.
219-2026: Authorizing the execution and delivery of a Project Agreement with NJDEP pursuant to the State Fiscal Year
2026 New Jersey Water Bank Financing Program.
