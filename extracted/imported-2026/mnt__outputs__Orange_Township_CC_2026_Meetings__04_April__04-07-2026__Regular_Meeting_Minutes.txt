City of Orange Township – City Council
 Regular Meeting Minutes – April 7, 2026
MINUTES CITY COUNCIL – CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP, NEW JERSEY
THIS IS THE REGULAR MEETING OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP HELD AT CITY
HALL, 29 NORTH DAY STREET, ORANGE, NEW JERSEY ON TUESDAY, APRIL 7, 2026 FOLLOWING THE
CONFERENCE MEETING.
ROLL CALL
Present: All seven Councilmembers present.
Also Present: Trisha A. Scipio, Acting Municipal Clerk; Chris M. Hartwyk, Business Administrator; Derrick Brown, Fire Chief;
Brian Mooney, Police Chief; Gracia R. Montilus, Esq. City Attorney; Marlin G. Townes, Esq. Legislative Research Officer.
APPROVAL OF MEETING MINUTES
March 17, 2026 Special, Conference and Regular Meeting Minutes adopted. Motion Adopted.
REPORTS
DEPARTMENT MONTHLY REVENUE COLLECTIONS: For March 2026, Clerk's Office collected $295.57, Public Works
collected $1,020.00.
OPRA REPORTS: There was no OPRA report.
CITIZEN COMMENTS
Aisha Samuels of 401 Highland Terrace raised questions about the Historic Preservation Commission, Highland Avenue
Train Station, paving schedule, water wells, Water Company, Parking Authority, permit parking, scattered site
developments, and the hospital site.
Alysha Rodriguez of 487 Mountainview Avenue expressed concerns related to rodent control, Historic Preservation
Commission, and walk-on resolutions.
Dr. Gloria Boseman of 345 Olcott Street congratulated the Radium Girls on the success of their March 25th event and
raised concerns about housing developments, community benefits, affordable housing, community engagement, local
advisory boards, and trust funds.
Margaret Cunningham of 400 Lincoln Avenue spoke about the need for improvements to the City's tennis courts.
Anna Mitchell of 33 Lindsey Place raised concerns regarding ongoing development within the City and the Council's
responsibility in it.
COUNCIL COMMENTS
Council Vice President Weldon M. Montague, III requested updates regarding the YMCA, parking meters, the Parking
Utility, the introduction of the municipal budget, and the anticipated completion date of the baseball fields.
Councilmember Clifford R. Ross raised concerns about Parking Utility personnel, police community engagement, e-bikes,
and spoke about a tennis clinic, proposed carnival, Seven Oaks gate, the reopening of Dunkin' Donuts, and a Town Hall
meeting on community benefits.
Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert spoke about the Historic Preservation Commission, the old Police Station, trash
concerns at 475 Lincoln Place, zoning hearings, and the Highland Avenue Train Station.
Councilmember Tency A. Eason shared that she attended the Homegoing Service for longtime former City Clerk Dwight
Mitchell. She noted that Washington Dodd has new ownership and will remain affordable housing, and spoke about the
paving schedule and enclosure of the tennis courts.
Councilmember Kerry J. Coley discussed the importance of hosting activities in the City's parks, ongoing development
projects, a petition to make New Street one-way, and parking concerns.

Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson expressed gratitude that her mother is doing well. She spoke about the Spring

ORDINANCES – INTRODUCTION / FIRST READING
3-2026: An ordinance authorizing online payment of property maintenance summons. Adopted on First Reading. Final
Reading on May 5, 2026.
4-2026: An ordinance to amend Chapter 200, Article VII to prohibit left turns (sponsored by Councilmember Clifford R.
Ross). Adopted on First Reading. Final Reading on May 5, 2026.
CONSENT AGENDA – RESOLUTIONS 194-2026 THROUGH 227-2026
194-2026 through 227-2026: Various resolutions including tax refunds, insurance renewal (Foundation Risk Partners Corp.),
attorney fee authorizations, NJ Department of Agriculture Summer Food Service Program grant ($203,041.09), fire
apparatus emergency funding, Resolution 203-2026 (rescinding Morton Salt addendum), Watermen LLC engineering
contract, Remington & Vernick extensions, ADG Contracting Corp change order, Mott MacDonald contracts (Lead Pipe
Replacement $1,214,000 + $200,000 for water utility services), Shauger Property Services emergency snow removal
($147,865.00), NJDEP grant application for Colgate Park, raffle license for Our Lady of Mount Carmel Church, Autism
Awareness Month, Child Abuse Prevention Month, National Garden Month, Emergency Temporary Appropriations #4, NJ
Water Bank Financing Program, Financial Literacy Month, National Library Month, National Volunteer Month, Alpine
Software Corporation payment ($15,626.51), medical/dental reimbursement ($3,420.18), University Hospital EMS contract
for 3 years commencing April 1, 2026, and the bill list (Grand Total: $43,247,227.83). Motion Adopted.
RESOLUTION REMOVED FROM CONSENT
199-2026: Memorandum of Agreement with Metro YMCA of the Oranges for 2026 Mayor's Circle of Excellence Summer
Camp, not to exceed $147,000.00. Adopted (4 Yeas, 2 Nays, 1 Abstain).
WALK-ON RESOLUTIONS
5-2026WO: AMENDED – Ordinance amending Chapters 88 and 200 to update Permit Parking Fees and establish a
Residential On Street Overnight & Commuter Parking Permit Program. Adopted on First Reading. Final Reading May 5,
2026.
228-2026WO: Grant application for NJ DCA Local Efficiency Achievement Program (LEAP) Shared Services Coordinator
Grant. Adopted.
229-2026WO: Change Order No. 1 for Well #5 Rehabilitation Project with Shauger Property Services, Inc., not to exceed
$86,181.18. Adopted (6 Yeas, 1 Nay – Ross).
RESOLUTIONS REMOVED FROM CONSENT
223-2026: Resolution rescinding proposed Ordinance No. 2 and reaffirming compliance with N.J.S.A. 18A:41-7.1 regarding

POSTPONED ORDINANCES
53-2025: No Parking Certain Hours (Central Avenue). Motion to postpone until information is received. Adopted.
ADJOURNMENT
Meeting adjourned at 9:19pm. Next meeting: Tuesday, April 21, 2026.
