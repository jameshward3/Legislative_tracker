MINUTES CITY COUNCIL – CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP, NEW JERSEY
THIS IS THE REGULAR MEETING OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP HELD AT CITY
HALL, 29 NORTH DAY STREET, ORANGE, NEW JERSEY ON TUESDAY, FEBRUARY 3, 2026 FOLLOWING THE
CONFERENCE MEETING.
Councilmember Kerry J. Coley led all assembled in the pledge of allegiance to the flag.
ROLL CALL
Present: Councilmember Kerry J. Coley, East Ward; Councilmember Tency A. Eason, North Ward; Councilmember
Quantavia L. Hilbert, West Ward; Council Vice President Weldon M. Montague, III, At-Large; Councilmember Clifford R.
Ross, At-Large; Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson, South Ward.
Also Present: Trisha A. Scipio, Deputy Municipal Clerk; Chris M. Hartwyk, Business Administrator; Derrick Brown, Fire
Chief; Brian Mooney, Police Chief; Gracia R. Montilus, Esq. City Attorney; Marlin G. Townes, Legislative Research Officer.
Absent: Council President Adrienne K. Wooten, At-Large.
APPROVAL OF MEETING MINUTES
January 20, 2026 Conference and Regular Meeting Minutes adopted. Motion Adopted.
REPORTS

Health Department $16,250.00, Planning Board $73,607.00, Parking Meters $15.01, Public Works $712,798.00.

COMMUNICATIONS AND PETITIONS
Notice of Public Hearings – New Jersey-American Water Company, Inc. (BPU Docket No. WR25110596).
CITIZEN COMMENTS
Alysa Rodriguez of 487 Mountainview Avenue expressed concerns regarding the Grant, Resolution Drafting, PVC railing,
Tax Sales, Legislative Research Officer, Citizens Comments, and Low-Income housing.
Dr. Gloria Boseman of 345 Olcott Street expressed concerns about new development impact, low-income capacity, the
hiring process, the library fundraising, and her obligation to question City matters.
Sheranda Volmar of 464 Valley Street expressed concerns about rodents and cats in the valley.
COUNCIL COMMENTS
Councilmember Tency A. Eason expressed appreciation to everyone who offered condolences on the passing of her
nephew and City employee, Ronald Mozell, Sr. She also addressed the importance of crisis management during the cold
season, including accessible warming centers and homeless shelters.
Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson requested that the City's snow removal process be published for residents.
She inquired about traffic mirrors for Scotland Road and Argyle Avenue (mirrors arrived and installation will be scheduled),
and the status of the traffic study for Claremont Terrace.
Councilmember Clifford R. Ross extended well wishes for a meaningful Black History Month and addressed the recent snow
removal efforts.
Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert extended well wishes for Black History Month. She expressed concerns regarding
immigration matters and addressed issues related to City-owned properties and snow removal.
Councilmember Kerry J. Coley raised questions regarding the Lincoln Avenue Traffic Study, snow removal equipment costs,
the Emergency Snow Removal Plan, and heating issues at the senior building at 400 Oakwood Avenue (Code Enforcement
