City of Orange Township – City Council
 Regular Meeting Minutes – February 17, 2026
MINUTES CITY COUNCIL – CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP, NEW JERSEY
THIS IS THE REGULAR MEETING OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP HELD AT CITY
HALL, 29 NORTH DAY STREET, ORANGE, NEW JERSEY ON TUESDAY, FEBRUARY 17, 2026 FOLLOWING THE
CONFERENCE MEETING.
Councilmember Jamie B. Summers-Johnson led all assembled in the pledge of allegiance to the flag.
ROLL CALL
Present: All seven Councilmembers present.
Also Present: Trisha A. Scipio, Deputy Municipal Clerk; Chris M. Hartwyk, Business Administrator; Derrick Brown, Fire
Chief; Brian Mooney, Police Chief; Gracia R. Montilus, Esq. City Attorney; Marlin G. Townes, Esq. Legislative Research
Officer.
APPROVAL OF MEETING MINUTES
February 3, 2026 Conference and Regular Meeting Minutes adopted. Councilmember Adrienne K. Wooten (who was
absent) abstained. Motion Adopted.

CITIZEN COMMENTS
Alysa Rodriguez of 487 Mountainview Avenue addressed OPRA requests, Fire and Police study, street paving, sidewalk
improvements, affordable housing, Resolutions 119-2026 and 126-2026, City's new insurance coverage, Scotland Road,
rodent concerns, and walk-on resolutions.
Dr. Gloria Boseman of 345 Olcott Street continued to raise concerns about new development, low-income capacity,
affordable housing, the hiring process, renters insurance, Trust Fund, and community engagement.
Jason Grove of Seven Oaks Road expressed appreciation to Department Heads for participating in the recent Community
Meeting and shared frustrations about ongoing traffic issues.
Kweli Campbell of 55 Randolph Place shared thoughts about Metcalf Park renovations, Open Space and Recreation
Committee, water bill increases, noise levels, and Community Benefits Agreements.
Antoinett Hall of 214 Duane Street expressed concerns regarding heating issues at 400 Oakwood Avenue, 300 Oakwood
Avenue, and 210 Central Place. Investigation is ongoing.
COUNCIL COMMENTS
Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert thanked Police, Fire, and DPW for snow removal. She expressed concerns regarding
the cleanliness of the City and enforcement of codes related to trash receptacles.
Councilmember Tency A. Eason thanked residents for patience during snow removal operations and urged caution
regarding black ice.
Councilmember Kerry J. Coley thanked residents for patience during snow removal and addressed a construction site not
maintaining proper cleanliness.
Council Vice President Weldon M. Montague, III inquired about the Recreation Center and Parking Utility. He requested an
update on parking meters from Main Street to Park Avenue, announced free tax preparation events for households earning
