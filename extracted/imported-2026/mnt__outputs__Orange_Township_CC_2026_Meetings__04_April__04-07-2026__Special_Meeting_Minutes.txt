PUBLIC SESSION
Tier A Municipal Stormwater General Permit – Watershed Inventory Plan (WIP) Presentation
The meeting presented the Tier A Municipal Stormwater General Permit and the City's Watershed Inventory Plan (WIP),
with Remington Vernick Engineer Terry Vogt explaining the permit context and the contents of the WIP. Mr. Vogt described
the WIP as a desktop analysis that integrates surface-water and watershed data, demographic and land-use information,
and newly digitized stormwater infrastructure datasets. The draft was prepared for filing before the New Jersey DEP
January 1, 2026 submission deadline and is being finalized now. A one-page fact sheet and his contact information were
provided. He offered to answer follow-up questions by email.
This session fulfilled the first of two required public hearings. No members of the public spoke during the hearing.
A motion to close the hearing was made by Councilman Clifford R. Ross and seconded by Councilwoman Quantavia L.
Hilbert. Motion adopted.
Mr. Vogt requested a second hearing in late June or early July to complete the public-hearing requirement and to
incorporate any changes before final submission to NJDEP and posting on the City stormwater webpage. Phase Two is due
the following January.
