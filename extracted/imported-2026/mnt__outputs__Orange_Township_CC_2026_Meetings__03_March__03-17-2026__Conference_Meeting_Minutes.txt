City of Orange Township – City Council
 Conference Meeting Minutes – March 17, 2026
MINUTES CITY COUNCIL – CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP, NEW JERSEY
THIS IS A CONFERENCE MEETING OF THE MUNICIPAL COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF ORANGE TOWNSHIP HELD AT
CITY HALL, 29 NORTH DAY STREET, ORANGE, NEW JERSEY ON TUESDAY, MARCH 17, 2026 AT 7:00PM.
The Local Girl Scout Troop led all assembled in the pledge of allegiance to the flag.
ROLL CALL
Present: All seven Councilmembers present.
Also Present: Trisha A. Scipio, Deputy Municipal Clerk; Chris M. Hartwyk, Business Administrator; Derrick Brown, Fire
Chief; Nile S. Clements, Chief Financial Officer; Gracia R. Montilus, Esq. City Attorney; Marlin G. Townes, Legislative
Research Officer.
The Acting Municipal Clerk announced a Moment of Silence. Councilwoman Tency A. Eason requested that all in
attendance rise in memory of Dwight Mitchell, former City of Orange Township Municipal Clerk.
MATTERS FOR DISCUSSION
Bond/Debt Overview
Everett Johnson of Wilentz, Goldman & Spitzer, P.A. provided an overview of how bonds and notes function. He discussed
the City's Bond sale, gross debt, net debt, net debt limit, and outstanding debt. He responded to questions from
Councilmembers Kerry J. Coley, Clifford R. Ross, and Tency A. Eason regarding primary risks to the City related to
bonding, the potential impact on the City's credit rating, and long-term effects on residents.
LEGISLATION DISCUSSION
The Acting Municipal Clerk announced the resolutions and ordinances to be presented at tonight's meeting.
Council Vice President Weldon M. Montague, III inquired about Resolutions 167-2026, 168-2026, and 169-2026 regarding
increases related to traffic control, off-duty police services, and diesel fuel.
Councilwoman Tency A. Eason inquired about Resolutions 159-2026 and 181-2026 pertaining to Integra and matters
involving the fiscal impact statement and the parking garage.
Councilmember Kerry J. Coley inquired whether the refund amount should be included in the title of Resolution 158-2026
and requested clarification regarding Resolution 160-2026 (Accurate Language Services).
Councilmember Clifford R. Ross inquired about Resolutions 178-2026, 184-2026, 185-2026, and 189-2026 addressing the
Historic Preservation Commission, benefits of a part-time Health Officer, funding source for that position, and the reason for
two separate Petry Engineering resolutions.
Councilmember Quantavia L. Hilbert inquired about Resolutions 178-2026 and 181-2026 regarding the compliance status of
the Historic Preservation Commission and payroll matters related to the Parking Authority.
Council President Adrienne Wooten inquired about Resolutions 165-2026 and 166-2026 (same company, different
products).
